## I-765
- **Current edition:** 08/21/25  
- **Accepted editions:** USCIS currently accepts ONLY the 08/21/25 edition. The official i-765 page lists a single "Edition Date 08/21/25" with no prior-edition grace list, so older editions are not listed as acceptable. (Note: an automated search snippet claiming a 02/01/26 edition plus a list of prior acceptable editions is INCORRECT/hallucinated — the live form PDF, its instructions, and the i-765 page all print 08/21/25 as of 2026-07-13.) All pages of a mailed paper form must show the same 08/21/25 edition date at the bottom or USCIS may reject it.  
- **Edition source:** https://www.uscis.gov/i-765
- **Fee (paper):** $520 general filing (initial, replacement, or renewal EAD) unless a reduced/exempt category applies  
- **Fee (online):** $470 general filing (a ~$50 discount vs paper; I-765 can be filed online for most categories)  
- **Biometrics:** $0 — there is no separate biometric services fee for Form I-765 under the current fee schedule. USCIS may still require a biometrics appointment, but no additional biometrics fee is charged.  
- **Fee notes:** Fees are from Form G-1055 Fee Schedule, Edition 05/29/26, Appendix C (I-765), currently in effect. Fee is determined by HOW the form is submitted (paper vs online) AND by eligibility category. KEY REDUCED/EXEMPT CASES: (1) If you filed Form I-485 WITH a fee on or after 04/01/2024 and it is still pending: $260 paper / $260 online. (2) If you have a pending I-485 filed on/after 07/30/2007 and before 04/01/2024 and you paid the I-485 fee: $0. (3) Replacement EAD due to USCIS card error or USPS/USCIS non-delivery: $0. (4) (c)(33) DACA: $520 paper / $470 online (plus the separate $85 Form I-821D fee). (5) Fee waiver (Form I-912) available to certain applicants: $0. POST-2025 PUB. L. 119-21 (H.R.1) ADDITIONAL FEE — a separate, NON-WAIVABLE fee submitted separately from the filing fee, applies to certain asylum/parole/TPS categories: INITIAL EADs for (a)(12) TPS, (c)(8) ABC-procedure asylum, (c)(11) parolee, (c)(19) TPS = filing fee ($520 paper/$470 online, or $0 for some parole/refugee subsets) PLUS $560; RENEWAL EADs for (a)(12) TPS, (c)(11) parolee, (c)(19) TPS, and (c)(34) = filing fee PLUS $280; RENEWAL (c)(8) asylum = filing fee PLUS $275. Numerous humanitarian/adjustment categories are $0 filing fee: (a)(3) refugee, (a)(5) asylee, (a)(7) N-8/N-9, (a)(8) COFA, (a)(10) withholding, (a)(16) T-1, (a)(19) U-1, (a)(20) U-2..U-5, (c)(1)/(c)(4)/(c)(7) diplomatic dependents, (c)(9) SIJ/VAWA/T/U/Iraqi-Afghan/CAA/HRIFA adjustments, (c)(10) NACARA/240A(b)(2), (c)(14) deferred action for U/SIJ/VAWA, (c)(25) T-2..T-6, (c)(31) VAWA, (c)(40) T bona-fide, and current/former U.S. armed forces service members. PAYMENT: USCIS no longer accepts personal/business checks, money orders, or cashier's checks for paper filings unless exempt — pay by credit/debit/prepaid card via Form G-1450 or by U.S. bank withdrawal via Form G-1650.  
- **Fee source:** https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/g-1055.pdf

### Eligibility categories (54 listed)
- **(a)(3)** — Refugee. File with a copy of your stamped Form I-94, Final Notice of Eligibility for Resettlement, or Form I-730-based approval. Fee-exempt ($0).
- **(a)(4)** — Paroled as a refugee. File with a copy of your Form I-94, passport, or travel document. $0 filing fee (Pub. L. 119-21 additional fee applies to some parole subsets).
- **(a)(5)** — Asylee (granted asylum). File with stamped I-94 showing asylee status, a USCIS/EOIR asylum approval, or Form I-730-based approval. Fee-exempt ($0).
- **(a)(6)** — K-1 nonimmigrant fiance(e) of a U.S. citizen, or K-2 dependent. Work authorized only during the 90 days in K-1/K-2 status; this EAD cannot be renewed.
- **(a)(7)** — N-8 (parent of an alien classed as SK-3) or N-9 (child of an N-8) nonimmigrant. File with evidence of lawful N status. $0 filing fee.
- **(a)(8)** — Citizen of the Federated States of Micronesia (CFA/FSM), the Marshall Islands (CFA/MIS), or Palau admitted as such under compacts with the U.S. $0 filing fee.
- **(a)(9)** — K-3 nonimmigrant spouse of a U.S. citizen, or K-4 dependent. File with evidence of admission and your K visa.
- **(a)(10)** — Granted withholding of deportation or removal. File with a copy of the EOIR immigration judge's signed order granting withholding. $0 filing fee.
- **(a)(11)** — Deferred Enforced Departure (DED). File with evidence of identity and nationality (or residence if stateless) and your basis for DED coverage.
- **(a)(12)** — Temporary Protected Status (TPS) granted. File with Form I-821 or evidence of approved/pending TPS. Filing fee $520 paper/$470 online PLUS Pub. L. 119-21 additional fee ($560 initial, $280 renewal).
- **(a)(13)** — Family Unity Program under IMMACT 90 (section 301). Generally obtained via Form I-817; Form I-765 needed only to replace a non-expired lost/stolen/damaged card.
- **(a)(14)** — LIFE Act Family Unity (section 1504 of the LIFE Act Amendments). Obtained via Form I-817; Form I-765 needed only for replacement of a non-expired card.
- **(a)(15)** — V-1, V-2, or V-3 nonimmigrant. File with evidence of V status (approval notice or Form I-94), or concurrently with your V-status application.
- **(a)(16)** — Victim of a severe form of trafficking — T-1 nonimmigrant. File with evidence of T-1 status (e.g., approval notice) for renewal/replacement. $0 filing fee.
- **(a)(17)** — Spouse of an E-1 treaty trader, E-2 treaty investor, or E-3 specialty occupation professional from Australia. File with evidence of your and your spouse's lawful E status plus marriage certificate.
- **(a)(18)** — Spouse of an L-1 intracompany transferee. File with evidence of your and your spouse's lawful L status plus marriage certificate.
- **(a)(19)** — U-1 nonimmigrant. Employment authorized incident to status; file Form I-765 for the EAD (or renewal/replacement) with evidence of U-1 status. $0 filing fee.
- **(a)(20)** — U-2, U-3, U-4, or U-5 nonimmigrant (derivative U). File with proof of derivative U status. $0 filing fee.
- **(a)(2)** — Legalization temporary resident under INA section 245A or 210 (approved I-687 or I-700). Form I-765 needed only to replace a non-expired card.
- **(c)(1)** — Dependent of an A-1 or A-2 foreign government official. Submit with Form I-566 through your diplomatic mission to the Department of State. $0 filing fee.
- **(c)(2)** — Dependent (spouse or unmarried dependent son/daughter) of a TECRO E-1 employee of the Taipei Economic and Cultural Representative Office. File with certification from the American Institute in Taiwan. $0 filing fee.
- **(c)(3)(A)** — F-1 student seeking pre-completion Optional Practical Training (OPT). File up to 90 days before completing one full academic year, with DSO-endorsed Form I-20.
- **(c)(3)(B)** — F-1 student seeking post-completion OPT. File up to 90 days before and no later than 60 days after program end date; must file within 30 days of the DSO OPT recommendation in SEVIS.
- **(c)(3)(C)** — F-1 STEM student seeking the 24-month STEM OPT extension. File up to 90 days before current OPT expires; requires degree evidence, E-Verify employer name and E-Verify Company ID (Form I-765 items 28.a-28.c); file within 60 days of the DSO recommendation.
- **(c)(3)(ii)** — F-1 student offered off-campus employment under the sponsorship of a qualifying international organization. File with the organization's certification letter and DSO-completed Form I-20.
- **(c)(3)(iii)** — F-1 student seeking off-campus employment due to severe economic hardship caused by unforeseen circumstances. File with DSO-certified Form I-20 and hardship evidence.
- **(c)(4)** — Dependent of a G-1, G-3, or G-4 nonimmigrant. Submit with Form I-566 through your international organization to DOS (UN missions via USUN). $0 filing fee.

- **(c)(6)** — M-1 student seeking post-completion OPT after completing studies. File with DSO-endorsed Form I-20 together with Form I-539; one month of OPT per four months of full-time M-1 study.

Renewal $520 paper/$470 online PLUS $275 Pub. L. 119-21 additional fee; ABC-procedure and certain pending-I-589 initial filings have differing fee/additional-fee treatment. Item 30 asks about arrests/convictions.
- **(c)(9)** — Applicant for adjustment of status under INA section 245 with a pending Form I-485 (also covers National Interest Waiver physician renewals, SIJ, T/U/VAWA adjustments, Iraqi/Afghan, CAA, HRIFA). Fee is $0 if the I-485 was filed with fee before 04/01/2024, $260 if filed with fee on/after 04/01/2024. Do NOT use (c)(9) if you are a refugee (use a3) or asylee (use a5) adjusting under INA 209.
- **(c)(10)** — Applicant for suspension of deportation or cancellation of removal (NACARA 203 and non-NACARA), and abused spouses/children under NACARA or INA 240A(b)(2). Many subsets $0 filing fee.
- **(c)(11)** — Parolee — paroled into the U.S. for urgent humanitarian reasons or significant public benefit (includes IMMVI military members/veterans and certain Ukrainian parolees). File with valid unexpired Form I-94/passport/travel document. Filing fee varies ($0 or $520/$470) PLUS Pub. L. 119-21 additional fee ($560 initial / $280 renewal) for most parolees; not available for certain expedited-removal cases.
- **(c)(12)** — Spouse of an E-2 CNMI investor (E-2C). EAD valid only for employment in the CNMI; requires marriage and CNMI-residence documentation and spouse's Long-Term Business/Foreign Investment Certificate.
- **(c)(14)** — Deferred action (including deferred action filed by U-status petitioners, Special Immigrant Juveniles, VAWA self-petitioners, and A-3/G-5 domestic workers with a pending civil action against an abusive employer). Requires evidence of the deferred-action grant and economic necessity (Form I-765WS). Many subsets $0 filing fee.
- **(c)(16)** — Registry applicant based on continuous residence in the U.S. since January 1, 1972. File together with Form I-485 or with evidence the I-485 is pending.
- **(c)(17)(i)** — B-1 nonimmigrant who is the personal or domestic servant of a nonimmigrant employer. File with evidence of B status, employer's nonimmigrant status, and prior/qualifying employment.
- **(c)(17)(ii)** — B-1 nonimmigrant domestic servant of a U.S. citizen who has a permanent home abroad or is stationed outside the U.S. and is temporarily visiting.
- **(c)(17)(iii)** — B-1 nonimmigrant employed by a foreign airline engaged in international transportation who would otherwise qualify for E status.
- **(c)(18)** — Alien with a final order of deportation or removal (including those granted deferral of removal under the Convention Against Torture) under an order of supervision. File with the EOIR order and Form I-220B.
- **(c)(19)** — Prima facie eligible TPS applicant with a pending Form I-821 (temporary benefit under 8 CFR part 244). Filing fee PLUS Pub. L. 119-21 additional fee ($560 initial / $280 renewal).
- **(c)(20)** — Applicant for legalization under INA section 210 with a pending Form I-700 (Special Agricultural Worker).
- **(c)(22)** — Applicant for legalization under INA section 245A with a pending Form I-687.
- **(c)(24)** — LIFE Legalization applicant (CSS, LULAC, or Zambrano class member) with a pending Form I-485 filed before October 1, 2000.
- **(c)(25)** — T-2, T-3, T-4, T-5, or T-6 nonimmigrant (derivative T). File with proof of derivative T status. $0 filing fee.
- **(c)(26)** — Spouse of an H-1B nonimmigrant (H-4) eligible under AC21 sections 106(a)/(b). Requires H-4 status proof, marriage proof, and basis for work authorization (approved I-140 or AC21 extension). Provide H-1B spouse's I-129 receipt number in item 29.
- **(c)(31)** — VAWA self-petitioner — principal beneficiary or derivative child of an approved Form I-360 filed as a VAWA self-petition, residing in the U.S. $0 filing fee.
- **(c)(33)** — Consideration of Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA). Must be filed with Form I-821D and Form I-765WS (economic necessity). Filing fee $520 paper/$470 online plus the separate $85 Form I-821D fee.
- **(c)(34)** — Paroled spouse of a (b)(37) entrepreneur (International Entrepreneur Rule). Filing fee $520 PLUS Pub. L. 119-21 additional fee ($560 initial / $280 renewal).
- **(c)(35)** — Principal beneficiary of an approved employment-based immigrant petition (approved Form I-140) facing compelling circumstances, in valid E-3/H-1B/H-1B1/O-1/L-1 status, no immigrant visa currently available. Renewal requires filing before current EAD expires; item 31.a asks for the I-140 receipt number and item 31.b asks about arrests/convictions.
- **(c)(36)** — Spouse or unmarried child of a principal beneficiary who has an approved Form I-140 and compelling-circumstances employment authorization under (c)(35). Provide the spouse's/parent's I-140 receipt number in item 31.a.
- **(c)(37)** — Applicant for CNMI Long-Term Resident Status. Must be filed together with Form I-955; if approved, work authorization is incident to status in the CNMI.
- **(c)(40)** — Bona Fide Determination Process for T nonimmigrant status principal applicants and eligible family members living in the U.S. (deferred action + bona fide determination EAD). $0 filing fee. Do not file a new I-765 if you already filed under (a)(16)/(c)(25) with your I-914 — that filing is converted.

### Key fields
- Part Part 1 / 1.a / 1.b / 1.c — **Reason for Applying (select only one): 1.a Initial permission to accept employment; 1.b Replacement of lost/stolen/damaged EAD or correction NOT due to USCIS error; 1.c Renewal of my permission to accept employment** [checkbox-set]: Exactly one box. For RENEWAL, select 1.c and attach a copy of your previous EAD. (Replacement/correction due to USCIS error does not require a new I-765 or fee.)
- Part Part 2 / 1.a-1.c — **Your Full Legal Name — Family Name (Last), Given Name (First), Middle Name** [free-text]: Provide full legal name exactly as shown on your birth certificate or legal change-of-name document.
- Part Part 2 / 2.a-4.c — **Other Names Used (aliases, maiden name, nicknames) — up to three sets of Family/Given/Middle** [free-text]: List all other names ever used; submit evidence (birth/marriage/divorce docs, government ID, passport identity page). Overflow goes in Part 6.
- Part Part 2 / 5.a-5.f — **Your U.S. Mailing Address — In Care Of Name; Street Number and Name; Apt/Ste/Flr; City or Town; State; ZIP Code** [free-text]: Must be a valid U.S. address (residence, APO, or commercial; a U.S. PO Box is allowed). Use In Care Of Name if mail goes to someone else. VAWA/T/U applicants may use a safe mailing address. USCIS mails the EAD here.
- Part Part 2 / 6 — **Is your current mailing address the same as your physical address?** [checkbox-set]: Yes/No. If No, complete the U.S. Physical Address in items 7.a-7.e.
- Part Part 2 / 7.a-7.e — **U.S. Physical Address — Street Number and Name; Apt/Ste/Flr; City or Town; State; ZIP Code** [free-text]: Required only if physical address differs from mailing address.
- Part Part 2 / 8 — **Alien Registration Number (A-Number) (if any)** [masked-format]: Enter as A- followed by the digits (form provides a fixed set of boxes for 8 or 9 digits). Begins with 'A'; found on DHS/USCIS correspondence. If you have none, type or print 'N/A'. Optional for a first-time initial applicant, but most renewal (EAD-holding) filers already have an A-Number and should provide it.
- Part Part 2 / 9 — **USCIS Online Account Number (OAN) (if any)** [masked-format]: You only have an OAN if you previously filed a form with a receipt number beginning 'IOE'. Not the same as an A-Number. Leave blank/none if not applicable.
- Part Part 2 / 10 — **Sex** [checkbox-set]: Male or Female, as shown on your birth certificate issued at (or closest to) the time of birth, or secondary evidence.
- Part Part 2 / 11 — **Marital Status** [checkbox-set]: Single / Married / Divorced / Widowed — the status you have on the date you file.
- Part Part 2 / 12 — **Have you previously filed Form I-765?** [checkbox-set]: Yes/No. A renewal filer answers Yes and should provide copies of prior EADs if available.
- Part Part 2 / 13 — **Provide your Social Security number (SSN) (if known)** [masked-format]: 9-digit U.S. SSN, only if known/issued. Optional. (This 08/21/25 edition has NO separate SSN-card request/consent items — only this 'if known' field.)
- Part Part 2 / 14.a-14.b — **Your Country or Countries of Citizenship or Nationality** [free-text]: List every country where you are currently a citizen/national (not necessarily birth country). If none, print 'stateless' and explain in Part 6.
- Part Part 2 / 15.a-15.c — **Place of Birth — City/Town/Village; State/Province; Country of Birth** [free-text]: Country of birth = the country's name as it was called when you were born, even if it has since changed or no longer exists.
- Part Part 2 / 16 — **Date of Birth** [date]: mm/dd/yyyy format (e.g., October 5, 1967 = 10/05/1967).
- Part Part 2 / 17 — **Form I-94 Arrival-Departure Record Number (if any)** [masked-format]: Enter your I-94 (Departure) number; obtain an electronic I-94 at cbp.gov/i94 if admitted after 04/30/2013.
- Part Part 2 / 18-21 — **Passport Number; Travel Document Number (if any); Country that issued passport/travel document; Expiration Date** [free-text]: Enter passport/travel-document info even if currently expired. Expiration date in mm/dd/yyyy.
- Part Part 2 / 22 — **Date of Your Last Arrival Into the U.S., On or About** [date]: mm/dd/yyyy.
- Part Part 2 / 23-25 — **Place of Last Arrival; Immigration Status at Last Arrival; Current Immigration Status or Category** [free-text]: Use the letter/number of the status (e.g., 'B-2 visitor', 'F-1 student', 'parolee', 'deferred action', or 'no status').
- Part Part 2 / 26 — **SEVIS Number (if any)** [masked-format]: Enter as N- followed by the SEVIS digits if you were issued one (students/exchange visitors).
- Part Part 2 / 27 — **Eligibility Category** [picker(known-set)]: Enter exactly one category as letter+number(s) in the three-box format, e.g. (a)(8) or (c)(17)(iii). Must be a valid code from the Who May File list. This drives the correct fee.
- Part Part 2 / 28.a-28.c — **(c)(3)(C) STEM OPT: Degree; Employer's Name as listed in E-Verify; Employer's E-Verify Company ID or valid E-Verify Client Company ID** [free-text]: Complete only if item 27 is (c)(3)(C).
- Part Part 2 / 29 — **(c)(26) Eligibility Category: receipt number of H-1B spouse's most recent Form I-797 for Form I-129** [masked-format]: Complete only if item 27 is (c)(26). USCIS receipt number (3 letters + 10 digits).
- Part Part 2 / 30 — **(c)(8) Eligibility Category: Have you EVER been arrested for and/or convicted of any crime?** [checkbox-set]: Yes/No. Complete only if item 27 is (c)(8). If Yes, follow the Special Filing Instructions for pending-asylum applicants and provide court dispositions.
- Part Part 2 / 31.a-31.b — **(c)(35)/(c)(36) Eligibility Category: Form I-797 (I-140) receipt number; and Have you EVER been arrested for and/or convicted of any crime?** [masked-format]: Complete only if item 27 is (c)(35) or (c)(36). 31.a = I-140 receipt number; 31.b Yes/No on arrests/convictions (do not answer Yes for minor non-alcohol/non-drug traffic violations unless you were arrested).
- Part Part 3 / 1.a-2 — **Applicant's Statement (read English yourself / interpreter used / preparer used)** [checkbox-set]: Select 1.a or 1.b; select item 2 if a preparer was used.
- Part Part 3 / 3-5 — **Applicant's Contact Information — Daytime Telephone; Mobile Telephone (if any); Email (if any)** [free-text]: Provide daytime phone; mobile and email optional.
- Part Part 3 / 6 — **ABC settlement box — Salvadoran or Guatemalan national eligible under the ABC settlement agreement** [checkbox-set]: Select only if filing under (c)(8) as an ABC class member; triggers 60-day decision commitment.
- Part Part 3 / 7.a-7.b — **Applicant's Signature and Date of Signature** [date]: Handwritten ink signature required (a photocopy/fax/scan of the ink signature is acceptable). A stamped or typewritten name is NOT accepted; an unsigned form is rejected. Date in mm/dd/yyyy. Must be filed while in the U.S.
- Part Part 6 / 1.a-7.d — **Additional Information (overflow) — Name, A-Number, and Page/Part/Item references** [free-text]: Use for any overflow; put your name and A-Number (if any) at top of each added sheet and reference the Page/Part/Item numbers; sign and date each added sheet.

### Document requirements
- A copy of your previous Employment Authorization Document (EAD, Form I-766) — _when:_ Filing a RENEWAL (Part 1, item 1.c). The form itself instructs: 'Attach a copy of your previous employment authorization document.' (https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/i-765.pdf)
- Correct filing fee (and, where applicable, the separate non-waivable Pub. L. 119-21 additional fee) per Form G-1055; or Form I-912 fee-waiver request for eligible applicants — _when:_ Every filing; amount depends on eligibility category and paper-vs-online submission. The additional Pub. L. 119-21 fee applies to certain asylum/parole/TPS renewals ((a)(12), (c)(8), (c)(11), (c)(19), (c)(34)). (https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/g-1055.pdf)
- Two identical passport-style color photographs are traditionally required with I-765; USCIS may also require a biometrics appointment (fingerprints, photo, signature) with no separate biometrics fee — _when:_ As required by the category/biometrics notice. USCIS may require biometrics at any time to verify identity and run background checks. (https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/i-765instr.pdf)
- Category-specific evidence of eligibility (e.g., DSO-endorsed Form I-20 for F-1 OPT categories; Form DS-2019 for J-2; Form I-821/TPS approval for (a)(12)/(c)(19); Form I-94/passport/travel document for parolees; approval or receipt notices, marriage certificates, and I-140/I-485 receipt notices for dependent and adjustment categories) — _when:_ Depends on the eligibility category selected in item 27 — see the 'Who May File Form I-765' section of the instructions for the exact list per category. (https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/i-765instr.pdf)
- Certified copies of arrest reports, court dispositions, and sentencing documents — _when:_ Categories (c)(8), (c)(35), or (c)(36) if you answer Yes to the arrest/conviction question, or if you were arrested for any traffic offense (item 31.b). Must be provided even if records were sealed, expunged, or cleared. (https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/i-765instr.pdf)
- Form I-765WS (Form I-765 Worksheet) demonstrating economic necessity — _when:_ Categories based on deferred action requiring economic necessity: (c)(14) deferred action and (c)(33) DACA. (https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/i-765instr.pdf)
- Full English translation with translator's certification for any foreign-language document — _when:_ Whenever any submitted supporting document is in a language other than English. (https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/i-765instr.pdf)
- Legible photocopies of documents (not originals) unless an original is specifically requested; all form pages must be included even if unanswered — _when:_ Every filing. Submitting unrequested originals risks USCIS destroying them. (https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/i-765instr.pdf)

### Validation notes
- Edition control: the current and only listed acceptable edition is 08/21/25. When printing a paper form, the 08/21/25 edition date and page numbers must be visible at the bottom of every page and all pages must be from the same edition, or USCIS may reject the form.
- Full legal name: provide it exactly as shown on your birth certificate or legal change-of-name document (Part 2, items 1.a-1.c). The I-765 instructions give no special rule for accented letters, hyphens, or apostrophes — reproduce the name as it appears on the underlying legal document.
- A-Number: begins with 'A' and is entered in the boxed A- field (8 or 9 digits). It is optional ('if any'); if you do not have one, type or print 'N/A'. It is used to identify your immigration records — most renewal filers who already hold an EAD will have one and should enter it.
- USCIS Online Account Number is distinct from the A-Number: you only have an OAN if you previously filed a form with a receipt number beginning 'IOE'; otherwise leave it blank.
- Dates: all dates use mm/dd/yyyy format (example given in the instructions: October 5, 1967 = 10/05/1967).
- ZIP Code: standard U.S. ZIP entered in the ZIP Code field; the form links to the USPS ZIP Code Lookup. Mailing address must be a valid U.S. address and a U.S. PO Box is permitted.
- Country of birth (item 15.c): use the country's name as it was called when you were born, even if the name has changed or the country no longer exists. Country of citizenship (item 14) may differ from country of birth; if stateless, print 'stateless' and explain in Part 6.
- 'N/A' vs 'None': for questions that don't apply, type 'N/A'; for numeric answers that are zero/none, type 'None' unless otherwise directed.
- Signature: a handwritten ink signature is mandatory; a stamped or typewritten name is not accepted and an unsigned form is rejected. A photocopy/fax/scan of the original ink signature is acceptable for filing. You must file Form I-765 while physically in the United States.
- Safe address: VAWA self-petitioners and T/U crime victims may list a safe mailing address (PO Box, friend's, attorney's, or CBO address) instead of their home address; the EAD is mailed to the item 5 mailing address.
- Eligibility category (item 27) must be a single valid code entered in the (letter)(number) box format, e.g., (a)(8) or (c)(17)(iii); the chosen code determines the correct fee and any additional Pub. L. 119-21 fee.

### Part 2, Item 1 — "My status is" (checkbox set, in printed order)
1. Lawful Permanent Resident (Proceed to Section A.) — Part 2, Item 1.a. First checkbox. Selecting this routes the filer to Section A (Item 2.a.-2.j.) for the Reason for Application. Used by standard green-card holders renewing/replacing a 10-year card.
2. Permanent Resident - In Commuter Status (Proceed to Section A.) — Part 2, Item 1.b. Second checkbox. Also routes to Section A. For lawful permanent residents who reside in a contiguous foreign territory (Canada or Mexico) and commute to U.S. employment.

- All dates use mm/dd/yyyy format (DOB, Date of Admission, all signature dates). Note: the edition date at the page footer is printed in mm/dd/yy format (01/20/25).
- For any non-applicable text item, type or print 'N/A'. For a numeric answer that is zero/none, type or print 'None' unless otherwise directed.

- State fields use the 2-letter USPS state abbreviation; ZIP Code per the USPS ZIP Code Lookup (5-digit or ZIP+4). Province/Postal Code/Country fields are for foreign addresses only.
- USCIS will NOT mail a Permanent Resident Card to a foreign address — only a valid U.S. mailing address (exception: alien commuters residing in a contiguous foreign territory may provide a foreign mailing address).
- Height is entered in feet and inches (e.g., 5 ft / 09 in) — not meters/centimeters. Weight is entered in pounds — not kilograms; if unknown or under 30 lbs or over 699 lbs, enter '000'.
- Signatures must be handwritten in ink; a stamped or typewritten name is NOT accepted. An unsigned or invalidly signed application will be rejected. For applicants under 14, a parent or legal guardian may sign; a legal guardian may sign for a mentally incompetent person.
- USCIS accepts a photocopied, faxed, or scanned copy of an original application bearing a handwritten ink signature as valid for filing.
- Submit legible photocopies of requested documents unless the instructions specifically require an original (e.g., reason 2.d./3.d. requires the ORIGINAL incorrect card). If you submit originals when not required, USCIS may destroy them.

- All submitted pages must be from the same form edition (01/20/25) with edition date and page numbers visible at the bottom; missing or mixed-edition pages may cause rejection.
- Non-U.S. citizens must notify USCIS of an address change within 10 days of moving (via www.uscis.gov/addresschange); do not send change-of-address requests to the USCIS Lockbox.

Authoritative rule per Item 8 of the 08/21/25 form/instructions: the field is 'Alien Registration Number (A-Number) (if any)' — OPTIONAL; 'Provide your A-Number... It begins with an A...
