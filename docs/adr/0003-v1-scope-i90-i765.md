# Scope v1 to Form I-90 and Form I-765, excluding complex petitions

v1 supports only Form I-90 (Permanent Resident Card renewal/replacement) and Form I-765 (EAD — both initial and renewal) and explicitly excludes attorney matching, complex one-time petitions (I-130/I-485, asylum), forum/news, web, and monetization. These two are the highest-recurrence USCIS filings, which is what the retention thesis needs; supporting *initial* I-765 (not renewal-only) captures first-time filers who will later renew, serving that same thesis.

## Consequences

The data model must carry the initial-vs-renewal distinction on I-765 from day one, and "recurring renewals" marketing must coexist with first-time-filing support. I-130/I-485 are deliberate no-s despite being more common, because their complexity and low recurrence work against both the UPL boundary and the retention thesis.

## Amended (2026-07-01)

Scope unchanged; language updated. **Application** is the product unit (see CONTEXT.md) — "filing(s)" above reads as *applications*. The initial/renewal/replacement distinction is carried by the required `applicationKind` field on `applications`, and **replacement** is explicit in v1 alongside renewal and initial (Work Permit initial/renewal/replacement; Green Card renewal/replacement).

## Amended (2026-07-05)

The **forum/news** exclusion in the original decision above is **withdrawn**. This supersedes both that exclusion and the "Scope unchanged" assertion of the 2026-07-01 amendment, insofar as scope now expands beyond form preparation. v1 now includes two additional surfaces:

- **Community forum** — pseudonymous community profiles, posts, comments, and reporting with admin-only moderation and pagination (`communityProfiles`, `forumPosts`, `forumComments`, `forumReports`). Anonymous reading is allowed; posting, commenting, and reporting require an upgraded account — a new account-gated action that extends the ADR-0010 contextual gate. This stays inside the ADR-0004 boundary: the product itself never gives legal advice or implies USCIS affiliation, and user-authored posts are governed by a per-post "not legal advice" notice, community rules, and admin-only moderation (MASTER_PLAN M4-T3).
- **Recent news** — a read-only surface on the Assistant screen that caches and displays the latest news from official USCIS sources only, with source links, timestamps, and a stale-cache fallback. It does not scrape USCIS (it fetches and caches from official USCIS source feeds only) and makes no claim on USCIS's behalf.

The scope of the *form-preparation* product is unchanged: v1 still supports only Form I-90 and Form I-765 (initial/renewal/replacement per `applicationKind`). Attorney matching, complex one-time petitions (I-130/I-485, asylum), and **web** remain excluded.

**Reconciliation:** the original exclusion sentence also lists "monetization," which is stale — monetization is in scope via ADR-0011 (one-time Service Fee) and RevenueCat (REARCHITECTURE.md). Treat monetization and the forum/news surfaces as in scope; where the original exclusion list or the 2026-07-01 "Scope unchanged" note conflicts with this amendment, this amendment governs.
